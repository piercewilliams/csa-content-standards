# Trend Feature

**Platform:** Trend Hunter / B2C Channels
**Type:** Trend and self-improvement content
**Primary persona:** [§4.2 The Curious Optimizer]({{ "/docs/curious-optimizer" | relative_url }})

[⬇ Download this section](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/piercewilliams/csa-content-standards/main/docs/trend-feature.md){: .btn }

---

> **Scope:** This page documents editorial writing standards for Trend Feature content — pieces that surface emerging trends, tools, and behaviors for a self-improvement-oriented audience. These guidelines direct the CSA's general-style agent. CSA product-level behaviors (locked headline formula, URL pattern, tags) are not yet specified for this format — follow General Guidelines §1 as the default for all unspecified elements.

---

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Purpose

A Trend Feature surfaces an emerging trend, tool, or behavior before the Curious Optimizer has encountered it — and makes the case for why they should act on it now. Its primary purpose is to be informative, actionable, and motivating. The reader should finish feeling ahead of the curve and ready to do something with what they've learned.

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Content Framework
**(REQUIRED)**

Each Trend Feature must address all four points in sequence. The CSA uses this framework to shape how it generates and evaluates Trend Feature content.

- **Discovery — What It Is:** Define the trend, tool, or behavior in one clear sentence — no jargon, no dilution
- **Understanding — Why It Matters:** Explain the broader cultural or emotional resonance — why this feels timely or empowering right now
- **Evaluation — Who It's For:** Frame benefits around motivations, not demographics: "If you're optimizing for energy…" or "If you care about smarter routines…"
- **Action — How to Experience It:** Offer concrete ways to try it — habits, purchasing tips, thought processes, or tech recommendations

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Tone
**(REQUIRED)**

Smart, approachable, and curious. Encouraging but realistic — optimistic without hype, direct without dryness. Never condescending. The reader should feel empowered by new information, not lectured at.

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Writing Guidance

- Never assume age, gender, income, or region — language must be universal and psychographically aligned
- Lead with intent and motivation, not personal identity markers
- Emphasize what readers can do with this information — utility is the hook
- Surface trends, tools, or behaviors before mainstream pickup; mainstream framing misses the core driver
- Actionable insights over abstract concepts — practical steps, not theory
- Blend optimism with tangible benefit: optimistic without hype, direct without dryness
- Avoid framing that requires the reader to see themselves in a specific demographic box
- The piece should make readers feel advantaged — not just informed, but ahead

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: human-only -->
### What to Avoid

| Prohibited | Reason |
|---|---|
| Demographic assumptions (age, gender, income, region) | Psychographic-first persona — universal language required; any demographic framing excludes part of the audience |
| Abstract concepts without practical application | Utility is the core hook; readers need something to do with the information |
| Hype without substance | Optimistic but realistic is the required tone; overclaiming erodes trust |
| Already-mainstream content framed as emerging | The persona's core driver is feeling ahead of the curve — not catching up to it |
| Identity-based benefit framing | Frame all benefits around motivations and outcomes, not who the reader is |

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: human-only -->
### Pre-Publish Checklist

- Trend, tool, or behavior defined in one clear sentence — no jargon
- Why it matters now stated explicitly in the piece
- Benefits framed around motivation, not demographics
- Concrete action steps provided — not abstract recommendations
- No demographic assumptions in language or framing
- Tone: optimistic without hype, direct without dryness
- Named human byline — creator/first editor only
- All facts verified; all links point to reputable sources
- Hero image appropriate and non-generic
- AI disclaimer present (CUE sites) and "Created With AI" checkbox checked in CUE if applicable
- Human review and approval obtained before publishing
