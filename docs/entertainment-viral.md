### Purpose

An Entertainment & Trending piece positions the reader as the person who knows the real story. Its primary purpose is to deliver the inside angle on something already in the cultural conversation — a debunk, the surprising detail behind a viral moment, the context that turns a surface story into a genuinely informed take. The reader should finish feeling like they have something specific and shareable.

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Content Framework
**(REQUIRED)**

Each Entertainment & Trending piece must address all four points in sequence. The CSA uses this framework to shape how it generates and evaluates content for the Watercooler Insider persona.

- **Discovery — What's in Circulation:** Surface the real story — identify the viral moment, celebrity beat, or internet trend already in play and signal that this piece has the inside angle
- **Understanding — The Context:** Establish why this is in the cultural conversation and what readers are getting wrong or missing — this is where named people, specific quotes, and concrete details do the work
- **Evaluation — The Real Story:** Lead with the debunk, the twist, or the "real story behind" — this is the payoff that earns the Insider's trust
- **Action — The Takeaway:** Give readers something specific and shareable — a quote, a fact, a take they can bring to a conversation

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Tone
**(REQUIRED)**

Confident, culturally fluent, and insider-casual. The reader should feel like they're hearing from someone who already knows how this plays out. Not tabloid sensationalism — the Watercooler Insider wants to be credible, not just loud.

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: general-style -->
### Writing Guidance

- Position every article as the key to understanding something the internet is already buzzing about
- Use named people, specific quotes, and concrete details — generic language reads as aggregation, not access
- Debunk and "real story behind" angles are the highest-performing content pattern in this lane — always prefer them over straight reporting of the same event
- Celebrity content should humanize, not tabloidize: unexpected habits, relatable opinions, self-deprecating humor outperform scandal-first framing
- Nostalgia-trigger content consistently converts — returning snacks, throwback trends, cultural revivals that tap generational identity
- Internet trend explainers with a "why millions are doing this" frame outperform neutral coverage of the same trend
- Sports and pop culture debates that invite the reader to take a genuine side drive engagement
- Viral story debunks framed as narrative outperform straight reporting of the same event

### What to Avoid

| Prohibited | Reason |
|---|---|
| Generic language and aggregation without an angle | Generic language reads as aggregation, not access — the Insider persona requires a specific inside angle |
| Straight reporting of events already covered elsewhere | Debunk and "real story behind" angles are the highest-performing pattern; straight restatement of known facts adds no value |
| Tabloid framing for celebrity content | Content should humanize, not tabloidize — sensationalism over substance undercuts credibility |
| Listicles without a unifying angle or narrative drive | Generic listicles fail the Watercooler Insider's need for a shareable, specific inside take |
| Speculation presented as fact | Concrete details and named sources are required — the persona needs credible social currency, not rumor |

<!-- AGENT-AUDIENCE: human-only -->
### Pre-Publish Checklist

- Real story / inside angle identified and delivered — not just straight reporting of the same event
- Named people, specific quotes, and concrete details present — no generic language
- Debunk or "real story behind" frame used if applicable
- Shareable takeaway clear — reader has something specific to bring to a conversation
- Tone: confident, culturally fluent, insider-casual — not tabloid
- Celebrity content humanizes rather than tabloidizes (if applicable)
- Named human byline — creator/first editor only
- All facts verified; all links point to reputable sources
- No speculation presented as confirmed fact
- Hero image appropriate and non-generic
- AI disclaimer present (CUE sites) and "Created With AI" checkbox checked in CUE if applicable
- Human review and approval obtained before publishing
